My Journey

I'm a Mechanical Engineering graduate from Ashesi University with a passion for solving community problems through innovative design and biotechnology. My work bridges mechanical engineering, synthetic biology, and microfluidics to create solutions that matter to struggling communities.

As a MasterCard Foundation Scholar and three-time iGEM participant, I've led interdisciplinary teams on international platforms while maintaining a deep commitment to education accessibility and social impact across Africa. My approach combines rigorous technical engineering with human-centered design thinking.

My work spans from founding ProGeneBio, a biotech startup for local enzyme production, to developing point-of-care tuberculosis diagnostics for HIV-positive patients in rural clinics, and mentoring the next generation of engineers.
