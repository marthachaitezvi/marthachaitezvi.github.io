My Journey

I am a Mechanical Engineering graduate from Ashesi University with a passion for solving community problems through innovative design and biotechnology. My work bridges mechanical engineering, synthetic biology, and microfluidics to create solutions that matter to underserved communities.

I am a Mastercard Foundation Scholar and a three-time iGEM participant. I have led interdisciplinary teams on international platforms while maintaining a deep commitment to education accessibility and social impact across Africa.

My work spans founding ProGeneBio, a biotech startup for local enzyme production, developing point-of-care tuberculosis diagnostics for HIV-positive patients in rural clinics, and mentoring the next generation of engineers through STEM education initiatives in Ghana.
