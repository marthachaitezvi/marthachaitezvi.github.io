Designed and developed a portable tuberculosis diagnostic device combining microfluidics and lateral flow assays for resource-limited settings. This research addressed the critical need for rapid, affordable TB diagnostics in rural clinics serving HIV-positive patients.

  • Laser cutting and molding fabrication techniques
  • DC motor-driven rotating chamber at 4000 rpm
  • 8% NaOH viscosity-reducing agent for sputum processing
  • Aptamer-based detection with gold nanoparticle visualization

Mathematical Modeling of Ebola Transmission Dynamics

Computational Epidemiology | Public Health

Led an interdisciplinary team research project developing a comprehensive SEIR (Susceptible-Exposed-Infected-Recovered) mathematical model to analyze Ebola transmission dynamics in West African populations. This research provides quantitative evidence for public health policy recommendations.

Model Development

  • 18-equation differential equation system
  • Population dynamics incorporation
  • Vaccination status tracking
  • Condom usage parameterization
  • Quarantine and treatment intervention modeling

Sensitivity Analysis

  • Critical parameter examination of condom usage (lambda)
  • Vaccination compliance analysis (k2)
  • Unvaccinated population protection (sigma)
  • Influence on exposure and infection rates across populations

Key Findings

  • ‚úì Condom adoption among vaccinated: up to 27% exposure reduction
  • ‚úì Vaccination non-compliance: ~24% exposure increase
  • ‚úì Unexpected parameter interactions discovered
  • ‚úì Nonlinear effects on protective behaviors
  • ‚úì Complex interdependencies in transmission mechanisms

Scenarios Modeled

  • Baseline transmission (zero intervention)
  • Partial condom usage implementation
  • Vaccination-only scenarios
  • Universal condom use conditions
  • Combined intervention strategies

CFD-Based Battery Thermal Management Optimization

Computational Fluid Dynamics | Lithium-ion Batteries

Developed a comprehensive computational fluid dynamics study optimizing lithium-ion battery thermal performance through cell geometry and spacing design. This research provides critical insights for electric vehicle applications and stationary energy storage systems.

Cell Types Analyzed

  • Cylindrical cells: 18650, 21700, 32650
  • Prismatic cells
  • Pouch cells

Simulation & Modeling

  • MATLAB finite difference analysis
  • ANSYS Fluent CFD simulations
  • Heat generation distribution mapping
  • Temperature field visualization
  • Cooling efficiency comparison

Key Findings

  • ‚úì Cylindrical 21700 cells: Superior thermal performance
  • ‚úì Lower peak temperatures achieved
  • ‚úì More uniform heat dissipation
  • ‚úì Optimal inter-cell spacing: 4 mm

Cooling Strategy Comparison

  • Air cooling analysis
  • Liquid cooling optimization
  • Water-based cooling: Best performance
  • Optimal flow velocity: 0.4 m/s
  • Maximum temperature achieved: ~302 K
  • Uniform temperature gradients maintained
  • Hotspot elimination achieved

Validation & Recommendations

  • Grid convergence analysis performed
  • Literature validation completed
  • Recommendation: 21700 cells with 4mm spacing + liquid cooling
  • Enhanced battery pack safety, efficiency, and lifespan for EVs
