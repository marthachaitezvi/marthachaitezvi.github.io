Development of a TB Diagnosis Device using Microfluidics and Lateral Flow Assays

Biomedical Device | Diagnostics | Capstone

This project focused on designing a portable tuberculosis diagnostic device to address diagnostic challenges in resource-limited settings, particularly for HIV-positive patients. The device integrates centrifugal microfluidics and lateral flow assays to enable rapid, low-cost point-of-care testing. A DC motorâ€“driven microfluidic system was designed to process viscous sputum using an optimized NaOH-based viscosity-reduction step. Numerical simulations and mechanical validation confirmed the safe and reliable operation of the rotating system. The project won a technical excellence award.

Alginate Hydrogel Optimization for Agricultural Water Retention

Materials Science | Agricultural Engineering

This study investigated the effect of calcium chloride crosslinking concentration on the chemical and mechanical properties of alginate-based hydrogels for agricultural water retention applications. Controlled laboratory experiments were conducted. Hydrogel performance was evaluated through water absorption, release, retention, and mechanical testing, with statistical analysis using ANOVA, Tukey post hoc tests, and regression analysis. Results showed that increasing crosslinking reduced water absorption but improved retention and mechanical strength, with 3% crosslinking identified as the optimal balance. The findings highlight the potential of hydrogel optimization for water-scarce agricultural contexts.
