TB Diagnostic Device for Resource-Limited Settings

Technical Excellence Award 2025

HIV-positive patients in rural clinics lack access to rapid TB diagnostics, causing delayed treatment and higher mortality rates.

Designed and prototyped a portable, centrifugal microfluidics-based diagnostic device integrating aptamer-based lateral flow assays. Features a DC motor-driven microfluidic system optimized to process viscous sputum samples rapidly and affordably. The device won the best technical excellence award project in Mechanical Engineering in 2025.

Currently the project received funding and is under research to futher develop the device and publish a reserach paper

Alginate Hydrogel Optimization & Mechanical Design

Advanced Materials & Engineering

Hydrogel Project

Erratic rainfall due to climate change has caused drought in some parts of Africa and this had hindered farmers to grow certain crops. Hydrogels can be used to reduced this problem by supplying water to the crops as needed. She went ahead to investigated calcium chloride crosslinking effects on alginate hydrogel properties for agricultural water retention. Identified optimal 3% crosslinking concentration for water-scarce contexts with superior water retention and mechanical strength.

The project won a $200 research grant from The Provosts Office. The research paved way for ongoing research on hydrogels at Ashesi Univeristy.
